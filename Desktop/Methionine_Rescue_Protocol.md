Methionine Rescue Protocol ("Metabolic Shield") — Draft Clinical Protocol

Objective

To provide a safety-preserving methionine rescue schedule that protects healthy tissues from systemic hypomethylation during aggressive IDE397 + Sacituzumab Govitecan (Trodelvy) combination therapy in MTAP-deleted solid tumors.

Key parameters

- IDE397 dosing: 30 mg orally once daily (QD), continuous throughout cycle.
- Sacituzumab Govitecan (Trodelvy): 7.5 mg/kg IV on Days 1 and 8 of each 21-day cycle (DL2 dosing scheme).
- Methionine Rescue (bolus): 1.5 g/m^2 oral or IV methionine equivalent given Days 10–13 (4 days), timed to restore SAM/SAH in healthy tissue post-cytotoxic strike.

Rationale

- Tumor cells with MTAP deletion cannot salvage MTA into methionine, so exogenous methionine selectively restores SAM in MTAP-proficient host tissues.
- Timing the bolus to Days 10–13 follows the Day-8 Trodelvy cytotoxic insult and targets the window of maximal systemic methyl-stress.

Design

Phase: Phase 1/2 adaptive combination expansion (safety lead-in followed by expansion cohort).

Primary objectives
- Safety: incidence of Grade 3/4 neutropenia and serious adverse events related to combination plus rescue.
- Efficacy: ctDNA molecular response rate (MRR) at Day 21 (>=50% reduction from baseline).

Secondary objectives
- ORR (RECIST v1.1), DCR, PFS, OS, H3K20me2 flux as PD marker, pharmacokinetics of IDE397 and methionine bolus.

Eligibility (inclusion highlights)
- Histologically confirmed MTAP-deleted solid tumor (documented by PredicineCARE or CLIA assay)
- ECOG 0-1; adequate hematologic, hepatic, and renal function
- Prior therapies: allowed; no active uncontrolled infection

Exclusion (selection)
- Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic therapy
- Prior severe reaction to ADCs or methionine therapy
- Uncontrolled CNS metastases

Monitoring & Safety

- CBC with differential: baseline; Days 3, 7, 10, 12, 14, 21 of each cycle (adjust per clinical status).
- CMP, coagulation panels: baseline and weekly during rescue window.
- H3K20me2 (CTC histone mark): baseline, Day 2, Day 9 (pre-rescue), Day 11 (during rescue), Day 15, Day 21.
- ctDNA (PredicineCARE): baseline, Day 8 (post-ADC), Day 21.

Stopping rules

- Dose-limiting toxicity: any Grade 4 non-hematologic toxicity attributable to drug combination or Grade 4 neutropenia lasting >7 days.
- If >33% of first 9 patients experience DLTs, halt escalation and convene DSMB.

Statistical considerations

- Safety lead-in: 3+3 escalation to confirm tolerability with rescue.
- Expansion: n=40–60 MTAP-deleted patients to estimate MRR with 95% CI.

Data collection & regulatory

- CRFs will capture dosing, labs, AE grading (CTCAE v5.0), and sample times for H3K20me2 and ctDNA.
- IND package items: preclinical toxicology summary, PK bridging data, manufacturing specs for methionine bolus, rationale and simulation outputs from DNA-Lang v52.x.

Next steps

1. Finalize rescue dosing route (oral vs. IV) based on PK bridging and tolerability data.
2. Draft IND/CTA appendices and investigator brochure changes describing methionine rescue rationale and safety monitoring.
3. Initiate site selection prioritizing centers with PredicineCARE access and on-site mass-spec or CTC processing capability.
