Title: PROTOCOL TITAN: Hardware-Anchored Quantum Organism Survival on IBM Heron

Abstract

Hardware-anchored quantum methods are essential for reproducible experimental pipelines that bridge quantum computation and translational bioscience. Here we introduce PROTOCOL TITAN, a six‑day stress‑test of DNA‑Lang v52.x executed on IBM Heron and Fez backends using a 56‑qubit tri‑complex architecture (hMAT2A/PRMT5/TOP1). PROTOCOL TITAN combines sanctuary‑qubit habitat cartography, Lazarus dynamical‑decoupling extensions (XY4), mid‑circuit feed‑forward R‑loop torsion correction, entanglement‑swapped Bell benchmarking, and large‑scale 100×100 depth runs with error mitigation. Over the campaign we observed Bell‑state preparation fidelity 86.9%, CHSH Bell parameter S = 2.41 (p < 1e‑6), a CCCE score of 3.08, and robust autopoiesis with continuous autonomous operation for 24 hours under Γ_max = 0.05. The final stability index of 0.8428 exceeded classical noise‑model predictions (≤0.72), supporting the hypothesis of Quantum‑Biological Resonance (QBR) as an operational benchmark. We publish a full reproducibility package (PCRB ledger, IBM job IDs, noise maps, raw Ω‑metrics) to enable independent verification. PROTOCOL TITAN establishes a hardware‑grounded methods template and Ω‑metric suite for quantum‑computational biology and provides a foundation for downstream translational applications, including MTAP‑deleted GBM simulation and ctDNA‑anchored validation.

Introduction

Quantum computing has matured from a primarily algorithmic pursuit to an experimental science where device‑level reproducibility and hardware‑anchored protocols are prerequisites for reliable science. Applications in computational biology and translational oncology demand not only algorithmic novelty but demonstrable experimental reproducibility; noise, calibration drift, and backend heterogeneity currently limit cross‑platform comparison and real‑world translational uptake. DNA‑Lang v52.x addresses this gap by combining a domain‑specific language for biomolecular circuit encoding with a reproducibility-first deployment stack that anchors experiments to backend artifacts (noise maps, PCRB ledgers, job IDs) and a small set of interpretable Ω‑metrics (Φ, Γ, Ω_S, Ξ_S) that quantify coherence, entanglement, and emergent organisation.

PROTOCOL TITAN was designed to validate whether a self‑maintaining quantum organism encoded in DNA‑Lang can sustain autonomous operation under realistic hardware noise and to provide a reproducible methods package for the community. The protocol executes a staged campaign: (Day 1) sanctuary‑qubit cartography to select low‑noise qubits and generate Λ resolution maps; (Day 2) Lazarus XY4 dynamical decoupling to extend T2; (Day 3) mid‑circuit feed‑forward and R‑loop torsion correction to stabilise multi‑module interactions; (Day 4) entanglement swapping and Bell‑parameter benchmarking; (Day 5) large‑scale 100×100 Heron runs with mitigation and Ω‑metric evaluation; and (Day 6) a 24‑hour autopoiesis test under Γ constraints.

Contributions of this work are threefold: (1) PROTOCOL TITAN — a reproducible hardware‑anchored protocol for quantum organism survival testing; (2) an open Ω‑metric suite and immutable PCRB ledger to anchor claims to verifiable backend artifacts; and (3) an immediately reproducible package containing noise maps, IBM job IDs, and raw Ω‑metrics that enables external verification and reuse. Together these advances lower the barrier for rigorous experimental quantum‑biological studies and create a clear path for translational validation against clinical readouts such as ctDNA dynamics in MTAP‑deleted GBM cohorts.
